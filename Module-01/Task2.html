Mutton Kacchi Biriyani

Description

Today I teach you how to cook Bangladeshi Mutton Kacchi Biriyani at your home.In the below I write all the ingredients name and the cooking step.Thank you

ingredients for Mutton Marination

  • 1 kg mutton(bone-in, cut into biriyani chunks)
  • 1 cup thick yougurt(whipped)
  • 2 tbsp ginger paste
  • 1.5 tbsp garlic paste
  • 1.5 tbsp red chili powder(adjust to taste)
  • 1.5 tbsp cumin powder & 1 tbsp coriander powder
  • 1 tbsp garam masala powder
  • 1.5 tbsp cups cripsy fried onions(beresta)
  • half cup cooking oil(use the oil leftover from frying onions)
  • Salt to taste

For the Rice & Assembly :

  • 500 g premium Basmati rice(soak for 30 minutes, then drain)
  • 3 medium potatos, halved
  • half cup ghee(clariffied butter)
  • 0.25 cup warm milk mixed with a pinch of saffron strands
  • 2 tbsp kewra water
  • 1 tbsp rose water
  • Whole spices (3 green cardomoms, 1 balck cardamom , 1-inch cinnamon stick, 3-4 cloves, 1 bay leaf)
  • Aloo Bukhara(dried plums) and raisins(optional)
  • Wheat flour dough (for sealing the pot)

Instruction:

  1. Marinate the Mutton:

    In a large mixing bowl, combine the mutton with the yogurt, ginger, garlic, red chili, cumin,coriander, garam masala,salt, and half of your crispy fried onions (beresta). Add the oil. Mix thoroughly to ensure everypiece is coated,cover the bowl, and marinate in the refrigerator for at least 4 hours (overnight is best for maximum flavor).

  2. Prepare the potatos:

    Toss the peeled, halved potatoes with a pinch of turmeric, salt, and a dash of sugar. Shallow-fry in oil until golden brown on the outside but still firm. Set aside.

  3. Par-Boil the Rice :

    Bring a large, deep pot of water to a rolling boil. Add salt (the water should taste slightly salty), the whole spices (cardamom, cinnamon, cloves, bay leaf), and a few drops of oil. Drain your soaked rice and add it to the boiling water. Cook the rice until it is about 40-50% done (it should have a slight bite in the center). Immediately drain the rice and set it aside.

  4. Layer the Biriyani:

    In the heavy-bottomed pot you intend to cook the biryani in, spread the marinated mutton evenly at the bottom. Place the fried potatoes on top of the meat. Sprinkle some chopped fresh mint, coriander, and the remaining crispy fried onions (beresta) over the mutton. Gently spread the drained par-boiled rice in an even layer over the meat and spices. Drizzle the ghee, saffron milk, kewra water, and rose water evenly over the rice.

  5. Dum Cooking(The Seal)

    Roll your wheat flour dough into a long rope and press it along the rim of the pot. Place the lid on top and press down firmly to create an airtight seal.
